  • Jordan


    Introduction

    Understanding Jordanian customs and etiquette is essential for a fulfilling journey. This guide explores dos and don'ts to assist you in navigating Jordan respectfully. From greetings to cultural practices, embrace Jordanian traditions for an enriching experience.


    Dos for International Students in Jordan:



    • 1-Respect Local Customs: Dress modestly and be mindful of cultural sensitivities.
    • 2- Engage with the local community: Join clubs or organizations, participate in cultural events, and make an effort to learn about Jordanian culture and traditions.
    • 3- Learn Arabic: While many Jordanians speak English, learning Arabic can greatly enhance your experience and facilitate communication with locals. Consider taking Arabic language classes or practicing with native speakers to improve your language skills.
    • 4- Explore the country: Jordan is a diverse country with a rich history and stunning landscapes. Take the time to explore beyond your campus or city and visit iconic sites such as Petra, Wadi Rum, and the Dead Sea. Traveling within Jordan will allow you to better understand the country's culture and heritage.
    • 5- Respect academic and institutional rules: Adhere to the rules and regulations of your academic institution, including attendance policies, academic integrity guidelines, and campus codes of conduct. Respect your professors, classmates, and academic staff.
    • 6- Try local cuisine: Jordanian cuisine is delicious and diverse. Explore local markets, cafes, and restaurants to sample Jordanian specialties such as falafel, mansaf, and kunafa.
    • 7- Seek support when needed: Adjusting to life as an international student can be challenging at times. Don't hesitate to reach out to your university's international student office, academic advisors, or counseling services if you need assistance or support.


    Don’ts:



    • 1-Don’t disrespect local customs and traditions: Dress modestly and be mindful of cultural sensitivities.
    • 2-Don’t engage in excessive public drinking or partying: While alcohol is available in Jordan, public intoxication and excessive drinking are frowned upon. Be discreet and mindful of your behavior, especially in public spaces. 5-Don’t disrespect academic institutions or authorities: Show respect for your professors, academic advisors, and university staff.
    • 6-Don’t disregard modesty: Respect the conservative dress code in Jordan. Avoid wearing revealing clothing and dress modestly to show respect for local customs.
    • 7-Don’t engage in illegal activities: Respect Jordanian laws and regulations, and refrain from engaging in any illegal activities, including drug use, vandalism, or theft. Violating local laws can have serious consequences, including legal action and expulsion from your academic program.

  • Turkey


    Introduction

    Understanding Turkish customs and etiquette is crucial for a smooth trip. This guide explores dos and don'ts to help you navigate Turkey respectfully. From greetings to dining, embrace Turkish culture for an enriching experience.


    Dos for International Students in Turkey:



    • 1-Greet with respect: When greeting someone, use a firm handshake and maintain eye contact. Address people with their titles and last names unless otherwise instructed.
    • 2-Be polite and respectful: Politeness is highly valued in Turkish culture. Use "please" (lütfen) and "thank you" (teşekkür ederim) frequently in your interactions.
    • 3-Dress Modesty: especially when visiting mosques or rural areas. Cover your shoulders and knees, and avoid revealing clothing.
    • 4-Remove shoes indoors: It's customary to remove your shoes before entering someone's home or a mosque.
    • 5-Engage in Tea culture: Tea (çay) is an integral part of Turkish hospitality. Accept offers of tea graciously and engage in leisurely tea-drinking conversations.
    • 6-Use right hand for gestures: In Turkish culture, the left hand is considered unclean. Use your right hand for gestures, passing items, and eating.
    • 7-Learn Basic Turkish Phrases: While many Turks speak English, learning a few basic Turkish phrases like greetings and polite expressions can enhance your experience


    Don’ts:



    • 1-Don’t use left hands for gestures:In Turkish culture, the left hand is considered unclean. Avoid using it for gestures, passing items, or eating.
    • 2-Don’t insult or criticize: Refrain from making negative comments about Turkey, its culture, or its people. Show respect for local customs, traditions, and way of life.
    • 3-Don’t point with finger: Pointing with the index finger is considered rude in Turkish culture. Instead, gesture with an open hand or nod in the direction you're referring to.
    • 4-Don’t Touch people’s heads: Respect Jordanian laws and regulations, and refrain from engaging in any illegal activities, including drug use, vandalism, or theft. Violating local laws can have serious consequences, including legal action and expulsion from your academic program.
    • 5-Don’t discuss sensitive topics: void discussing sensitive topics such as politics, religion, or ethnic tensions, as these subjects can be divisive and lead to misunderstandings.
    • 5-Don’t waste food: urkish cuisine is celebrated for its richness and variety. Avoid wasting food as it's considered disrespectful to the host and to the food itself.
